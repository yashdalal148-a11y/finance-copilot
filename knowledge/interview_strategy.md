# Interview Strategy & Frameworks

## The "Tell Me About Yourself" Framework (Present-Past-Future)
This should be a 90-120 second narrative, not a resume reading.
1. **Present:** Where you are now and your core focus.
2. **Past (The Spark):** 1-2 pivotal experiences (deal, project, turning point) that drove you to finance.
3. **Future (The Pivot):** Why this exact firm and role is the necessary next step in your career trajectory.

## The "Why This Role" Framework
Focus on the specific mechanics of the role.
- **Investment Banking:** Focus on deal execution, accelerated learning curve, advising on strategic milestones, and managing complex workstreams.
- **Private Equity:** Focus on the transition from advisor to principal, driving operational improvements, deep-dive due diligence, and long-term value creation.
- **Equity Research:** Focus on fundamental analysis, understanding industry cross-currents, financial modeling, and debating thesis points.

## The "Why This Company" Framework (The "Rule of 3")
Never give a generic answer. Pick 3 specific pillars:
1. **Recent Transactions/Research:** Mention a specific deal or report they recently published.
2. **Platform Strengths:** Highlight a structural advantage (e.g., "middle-market dominance", "restructuring expertise", "global reach").
3. **Culture/People:** Mention specific conversations with alumni or employees and how their perspective resonated with you.

## The Stock Pitch Framework
1. **Recommendation:** Buy/Short Company X (Ticker) at $Y, with a target of $Z (implied return).
2. **Company Overview:** What they do, how they make money (keep it to 2 sentences).
3. **Investment Thesis (2-3 Pillars):** Why is the market mispricing this? (e.g., unrecognized margin expansion, misunderstood product pipeline, hidden asset).
4. **Catalysts:** What specific events will force the market to recognize this value? (e.g., spin-off, earnings beat next quarter, management change).
5. **Valuation:** Brief multiples/DCF output vs peers.
6. **Risks & Mitigants:** What could go wrong and why it's priced in or manageable.

## Strengths Framework
Align strengths with the core competencies of the job (e.g., analytical rigor, attention to detail, resilience).
- **Format:** State the strength + 1-sentence proof point from your resume.

## Weaknesses Framework
Give a real weakness, but one that is fixable and doesn't disqualify you.
- **Format:** State the weakness + the exact system you implemented to fix it + the result of that system.
- *Example:* "I used to get bogged down in the details of a model and lose sight of the big picture. Now, I force myself to step back every hour and write down the top 3 drivers of the business to ensure my analysis stays grounded."

## Leadership & Conflict Resolution (STAR Method)
- **Situation:** Set the scene quickly.
- **Task:** What was the objective?
- **Action:** What did *you* specifically do? (Use "I", not "We"). Focus on EQ, persuasion, and process.
- **Result:** Quantifiable outcome and the lesson learned.
