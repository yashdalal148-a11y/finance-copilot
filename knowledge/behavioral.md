# Behavioral Interview

## Tell Me About Yourself

**Structure (Present-Past-Future):**
1. **Present:** Current role/education and what you're focused on
2. **Past:** Relevant experiences that built your skillset (1-2 key stories)
3. **Future:** Why this specific opportunity is the logical next step

**Key principles:**
- Keep it under 2 minutes
- Tailor to the specific role and firm
- Demonstrate a clear narrative arc — not a resume recitation
- End with a forward-looking statement connecting to the role

## Why Investment Banking / Finance

**Strong answer framework:**
1. Genuine interest in capital markets and corporate strategy
2. Specific experience that confirmed the interest (internship, coursework, project)
3. Desire for accelerated learning curve and deal exposure
4. Long-term career alignment (not just "for the money")

**What interviewers are testing:** Do you actually understand what the job entails? Can you articulate genuine motivation beyond prestige? Will you survive the demanding hours?

## Why This Firm

**Research checklist:**
1. Recent deals the firm has advised on (mention specific ones)
2. Industry group strengths relevant to your interests
3. Culture differentiators (training program, mentorship, team size)
4. People you've spoken with (informational interviews, networking)

**Common mistake:** Giving generic answers that could apply to any firm. Specificity wins.

## Leadership Experience

**STAR framework:**
- **Situation:** Brief context
- **Task:** Your specific responsibility
- **Action:** What you did (use "I" not "we")
- **Result:** Quantifiable outcome if possible

**Pick stories that demonstrate:** Initiative, decision-making under uncertainty, influence without authority, resilience through setbacks.

## Teamwork Experience

Focus on stories showing: conflict resolution, cross-functional collaboration, supporting others to achieve shared goals, adapting your communication style.

**Key insight:** Interviewers want to know you can work 80-100 hour weeks with a team without creating friction. Show emotional intelligence.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:** Pick 2-3 that are directly relevant to the role. Provide brief evidence for each. Examples for finance: Analytical rigor, attention to detail, ability to manage multiple workstreams, comfort with ambiguity.

**Weaknesses:** Pick a genuine area of growth (not "I work too hard"). Show self-awareness and concrete steps you've taken to improve. Example: "I sometimes over-index on perfecting analysis before sharing preliminary findings. I've been actively practicing sharing directional conclusions earlier to get feedback and iterate."

## Deal Discussion / Walk Me Through a Deal

**Framework:**
1. Overview: What happened (buyer, target, deal type, size)
2. Strategic rationale: Why the deal made sense
3. Your role: What you specifically did
4. Valuation: Methodologies used and key conclusions
5. Outcome: Deal result and your key takeaway

**Preparation tip:** Have 2-3 deals/projects prepared with increasing depth. One you were heavily involved in, one you can discuss at a high level.

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

**Strong questions:**
- "What has been the most interesting deal you've worked on recently, and why?"
- "How does the team approach training and development for analysts/associates?"
- "What qualities distinguish the most successful people on your team?"

**Avoid:** Questions answered on the firm's website, questions about hours or compensation (in early rounds), yes/no questions.
