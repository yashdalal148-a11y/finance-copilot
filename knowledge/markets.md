# Markets

## Equity Markets

**Market structure:** Primary market (IPOs, secondary offerings) vs. Secondary market (exchange trading). Major exchanges: NYSE, NASDAQ, LSE, TSE.

**Order types:** Market orders (execute immediately at best available price), Limit orders (execute only at specified price or better), Stop orders (trigger market order when price reaches threshold), Stop-limit orders.

**Market participants:** Market makers (provide liquidity), institutional investors (mutual funds, pension funds, insurance), retail investors, high-frequency traders, dark pools.

**Indices:** S&P 500 (large-cap US, market-cap weighted), Dow Jones (30 blue-chips, price-weighted), NASDAQ Composite (tech-heavy), Russell 2000 (small-cap).

## Fixed Income

**Bond pricing:** Bond price = PV of coupon payments + PV of par value at maturity.

**Yield measures:**
- Current Yield = Annual Coupon / Current Price
- Yield to Maturity (YTM): Total return if held to maturity, accounting for price, coupon, and reinvestment
- Yield to Call (YTC): YTM but assuming call at earliest call date

**Duration:** Measures price sensitivity to interest rate changes. Modified Duration ≈ % change in price for a 1% change in yield. Higher duration = higher interest rate risk.

**Convexity:** Measures the curvature of the price-yield relationship. Duration is linear approximation; convexity captures the second-order effect.

**Yield curve shapes:** Normal (upward sloping — economy healthy), Flat (uncertainty), Inverted (rates higher at short end — recession signal).

**Credit spreads:** Difference in yield between a corporate bond and a Treasury of similar maturity. Wider spread = higher perceived default risk.

**Bond rating agencies:** Moody's (Aaa to C), S&P/Fitch (AAA to D). Investment grade: BBB-/Baa3 and above. High yield (junk): below BBB-.

## Derivatives

**Options:**
- Call option: Right to buy at strike price
- Put option: Right to sell at strike price
- American: Can exercise any time before expiry
- European: Can only exercise at expiry

**Put-Call Parity:** C + PV(K) = P + S (for European options)

**The Greeks:**
- Delta: Rate of change of option price with respect to underlying price
- Gamma: Rate of change of delta
- Theta: Time decay
- Vega: Sensitivity to implied volatility
- Rho: Sensitivity to interest rates

**Black-Scholes key inputs:** Current stock price, strike price, time to expiration, risk-free rate, implied volatility.

**Futures vs. Forwards:** Futures are standardized, exchange-traded, marked-to-market daily, and carry minimal counterparty risk. Forwards are customized, OTC, settled at expiration, and carry counterparty risk.

## Foreign Exchange

**Quote conventions:** EUR/USD = 1.10 means 1 Euro buys 1.10 US Dollars. The first currency is the base, the second is the quote.

**Interest Rate Parity:** Forward rate reflects the interest rate differential between two currencies. Higher interest rate currency trades at a forward discount.

**Purchasing Power Parity:** In the long run, exchange rates adjust so that identical goods cost the same in different currencies when converted.

**Key FX drivers:** Interest rate differentials, inflation differentials, current account balances, capital flows, geopolitical risk, central bank intervention.

## Macroeconomics

**Key indicators:**
- GDP growth (real vs. nominal)
- Inflation (CPI, PCE, core vs. headline)
- Unemployment rate and non-farm payrolls
- Federal Funds Rate (US monetary policy tool)
- Yield curve shape
- PMI (Manufacturing and Services)

**Monetary policy tools:** Open market operations (buying/selling Treasuries), Federal Funds Rate target, reserve requirements, quantitative easing/tightening.

**Fiscal policy:** Government spending and taxation. Expansionary (deficit spending) vs. contractionary (austerity). Automatic stabilizers vs. discretionary policy.

**Business cycle phases:** Expansion, Peak, Contraction (Recession), Trough. Different sectors outperform at different phases. Cyclicals (industrials, materials) outperform in early expansion; defensives (utilities, healthcare) outperform in late cycle and contraction.

## Commodities

**Major categories:** Energy (crude oil, natural gas), Metals (gold, silver, copper), Agriculture (wheat, corn, soybeans).

**Key concepts:** Spot vs. futures price, contango (futures > spot), backwardation (spot > futures), storage costs, convenience yield.

**Gold as an asset:** Traditional safe haven, inflation hedge, negatively correlated with real interest rates, central bank reserve asset.
