# Interview Questions Bank

## Valuation Questions

### Walk me through a DCF
This is the single most common technical question. The interviewer wants a structured, step-by-step walkthrough: project FCF, calculate terminal value, discount at WACC, bridge from EV to equity value, divide by shares for implied price. Emphasize key assumptions you would sensitise.

### How do you value a company with no earnings?
Use EV/Revenue multiples (comparable company analysis), or a DCF with a longer projection period that captures the path to profitability. For early-stage companies, consider VC methods (target return working backward from expected exit).

### When would you use a sum-of-the-parts valuation?
When a company operates in multiple distinct business segments with different growth profiles, margins, and risk characteristics. Each segment is valued separately using the most appropriate methodology, and the values are summed. Common for conglomerates.

### What is the difference between enterprise value and equity value?
Enterprise Value = total value of the business to all capital providers. Equity Value = value to equity holders only. EV = Equity Value + Net Debt + Preferred Stock + Minority Interest. Use EV with unlevered metrics (EBITDA, Revenue, EBIT). Use Equity Value with levered metrics (Net Income, EPS, Book Value).

### Walk me through an LBO
A financial sponsor acquires a company using a significant amount of debt (typically 4-6x EBITDA). The sponsor contributes equity (30-50%), uses the company's cash flows to pay down debt, improves operations, and exits in 3-7 years targeting a 20-25% IRR.

## Accounting Questions

### Walk me through the three financial statements
Income Statement shows profitability over a period. Balance Sheet shows assets, liabilities, and equity at a point in time. Cash Flow Statement shows actual cash movements. They are linked: Net Income flows to Retained Earnings on the BS and is the starting line of the CFS.

### If depreciation increases by $10, walk me through the impact on all three statements
Income Statement: Pre-tax income decreases by $10. Tax decreases by $10 × tax rate. Net Income decreases by $10 × (1 - tax rate). Cash Flow Statement: Net Income is lower, but D&A is added back, so CFO increases by $10 × tax rate. Balance Sheet: PP&E decreases by $10. Cash increases by $10 × tax rate. Retained Earnings decreases by $10 × (1 - tax rate). The balance sheet balances.

### What is working capital and why does it matter?
NWC = Current Assets (ex-cash) - Current Liabilities (ex-debt). An increase in NWC is a use of cash. Companies that grow quickly often need to fund working capital growth, which consumes cash even when profitable.

### What is deferred revenue?
Cash received before the service is delivered. It is a liability because the company owes the customer future service. As the service is delivered, deferred revenue decreases and revenue is recognized.

## Markets Questions

### If interest rates rise, what happens to bond prices?
Bond prices fall. There is an inverse relationship between interest rates and bond prices. The magnitude depends on duration — longer-duration bonds are more sensitive.

### Explain put-call parity
For European options: Call + PV(Strike) = Put + Stock Price. If this relationship breaks, arbitrage opportunities exist. This is a fundamental no-arbitrage relationship in options pricing.

### What is an inverted yield curve and why does it matter?
An inverted yield curve occurs when short-term rates exceed long-term rates. It has historically been a reliable recession predictor. It signals that the market expects future rate cuts due to economic weakness.

## Behavioral Questions

### Why investment banking?
Focus on intellectual curiosity about how deals are structured, desire for a steep learning curve, genuine interest in advising companies on strategic decisions, and relevant experience that confirmed these interests.

### Tell me about a time you failed
Pick a real failure. Show self-awareness about what went wrong. Emphasize what you learned and how you changed your approach afterward. Avoid trivial examples.

### Walk me through your resume
Use the Present-Past-Future framework. Highlight 2-3 key experiences, connect them to the role, and explain your trajectory. Keep it under 2 minutes.
