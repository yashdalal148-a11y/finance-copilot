# Equity Research

## Industry Analysis

**Porter's Five Forces framework:**
1. Threat of new entrants (barriers to entry)
2. Bargaining power of suppliers
3. Bargaining power of buyers
4. Threat of substitutes
5. Competitive rivalry

**Industry lifecycle stages:** Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline. The stage determines growth expectations, margin profiles, and appropriate valuation multiples.

**TAM/SAM/SOM analysis:**
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): Maximum revenue opportunity
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): Portion of TAM reachable with current products/distribution
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Realistic near-term capture

## Company Analysis

**Competitive advantage assessment (Moats):**
- Network effects (Visa, Meta)
- Switching costs (SAP, Oracle)
- Cost advantages (Walmart, Costco)
- Intangible assets (Disney brands, pharma patents)
- Efficient scale (utilities, railroads)

**Management quality evaluation:** Track record of capital allocation, insider ownership, compensation alignment, strategic clarity, execution history.

**Unit economics:** Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Lifetime Value (LTV), LTV/CAC ratio (>3x is healthy), payback period, gross margin per unit.

## Financial Modeling

**Revenue build:** Top-down (market size × market share) or bottom-up (units × price, or stores × revenue per store).

**Three-statement model:**
1. Build the Income Statement (revenue drivers, margin assumptions)
2. Build the Balance Sheet (working capital, CapEx schedule, debt schedule)
3. Build the Cash Flow Statement (links from IS and BS changes)
4. Create the balancing mechanism (revolver or excess cash)

**Model best practices:**
- Separate inputs, calculations, and outputs
- Use blue font for hard-coded assumptions
- Use black font for formulas
- Build in error checks (does the balance sheet balance?)
- Include a sensitivity analysis on key assumptions

## Stock Recommendations

**Rating framework:**
- Buy/Overweight: Expected total return > market benchmark + threshold
- Hold/Equal-Weight: Expected return approximately in line with market
- Sell/Underweight: Expected return below market

**Initiating coverage structure:**
1. Investment thesis (3-5 key points)
2. Business overview and competitive positioning
3. Industry dynamics
4. Financial analysis and projections
5. Valuation (multiple methodologies)
6. Risks to thesis
7. Price target derivation

**Price target methodologies:** DCF-derived fair value, target multiple × forward earnings/EBITDA, sum-of-the-parts, blended approach.

## Catalysts

**Positive catalysts:** Earnings beats, new product launches, market expansion, activist involvement, regulatory tailwinds, management changes, M&A activity, share buyback announcements.

**Negative catalysts:** Earnings misses, competitive threats, regulatory headwinds, key customer loss, management departures, debt covenant violations, dividend cuts.

**Catalyst timeline:** Near-term (0-3 months), medium-term (3-12 months), long-term (1-3 years). Match catalyst horizon to investment time frame.

## Earnings Analysis

**What to focus on in earnings calls:**
- Revenue growth (organic vs. acquisition-driven)
- Margin trends (expanding or contracting, and why)
- Management guidance changes (raised, maintained, lowered)
- Free cash flow generation and conversion
- Capital allocation priorities
- Forward commentary and tone

**Key metrics by sector:**
- Tech: ARR, NRR, Rule of 40
- Retail: Same-store sales, inventory turnover
- Banks: NIM, efficiency ratio, credit quality
- Healthcare: Pipeline progress, patent cliffs
- Industrials: Book-to-bill ratio, backlog growth
